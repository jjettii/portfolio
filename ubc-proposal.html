01Scope

Three brands, four audiences.

Focus splits 70 percent rental and owner-operator, 30 percent development. The development header is edited from existing building assets plus a small amount of new footage of the UBCPT team and client consultations. People lead every frame. Buildings and amenities sit behind them.

02Approach

How we shoot it

01

People first. Buildings as backdrop

Every frame leads with a person. The architecture and amenities provide context, not the subject. This keeps the work distinct from generic community stock.

02

Natural light. Minimal staging

Cast for believability over polish, so faculty read as faculty and students read as students. West-coast light, documentary handling.

03

One visual language across three brands

Consistent grade, framing, and pacing, so the three brand worlds connect without looking identical.

05Team

A small, senior crew

Jordan EttingerDirector

Jordan Ettinger is a director and video producer based on Vancouver Island, BC. He has been making videos for twenty years and has worked full-time in production since 2021, completing more than 150 projects across documentary, outdoor, sports, e-commerce, and luxury — collaborating with studios, independent brands, and individual clients across Western Canada and beyond.

His foundation is in narrative filmmaking. He got his start making short films and skateboarding videos, where he developed an instinct for pacing, movement, and how the shape of a story changes what a viewer feels. That sensibility carries directly into his client work today. His focus is production for the design and creative community — designers, architects, outdoor and hospitality brands, and creative studios who need films that hold up next to the work they're proud of. The result is content that is clean, considered, and built to last.

Ben GiesbrechtCinematographer

Ben Giesbrecht is an award-winning cinematographer with more than a decade of professional experience creating documentary, commercial, tourism, and branded content. Based on Vancouver Island and the founder of Hideaway Creative, Ben specializes in crafting authentic, human-centered stories that connect audiences to people, places, and communities.

His work has been recognized with a Leo Award for Cinematography, an Indie Award, and three victories at the Whistler Film Festival's 72-Hour Filmmaker Showdown. Over the years, Ben has collaborated with leading brands and organizations including BMW Canada, Finisterre, Purdys Chocolatier, ICBC, Destination BC, Tourism Vancouver Island, and numerous communities and businesses across Western Canada.

With a cinematic yet natural approach, Ben focuses on capturing genuine moments and real experiences, creating visually compelling content that resonates with audiences while staying grounded in authenticity.

Charles NasbyPhotographer

Charles Nasby is a freelance photographer based in Vancouver, BC. After working 6 years at local in-house studios (Aritzia Photo Studio and Herschel Supply Creative Team) he began his freelance journey in 2023. Since then, he has shot for numerous local clients including Tacofino, Marcon, Rize Alliance Properties, Fable Home and Michelin Star awarded Kissa Tanto Restaurant.

Charles prefers a natural tone and realism that highlights the emotion of a specific moment rather than an overly staged look. His goal is to make the viewer think, "I wish I were there right now" and to feel the subject in a relatable way. Whether it's a quiet space or a crowded gathering, there's always something to find desirable in his work.
